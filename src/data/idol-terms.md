# Idol Terms   
## 🌟 The "Oshi" & Fan Hierarchy   
Terms related to the idols themselves and the different types of fans.   
### Support Types   
- **Oshi (推し):** Your "favorite" or the one you support.   
- **Oshimai / Oshimen (推しメン):** Your favorite member within a specific group.   
- **Tan-oshi (単推し):** Supporting **only one** specific member.   
- **Hako-oshi (箱推し) / Zen-oshi:** Supporting the **entire group** equally.   
- **DD (Daredemo Daisuki):** "I love everyone." A fan who has many favorites across different groups.   
- **Tantou (担当):** (Common in Johnny’s fandom) Your "assignment" or the member you are responsible for supporting.   
    - **Ji-tan (自担):** Your own favorite member.   
    - **All-tan / G-tan:** Supporting the whole group.   
- **Kake-mochi (掛け持ち):** Supporting multiple idols/fandoms simultaneously.   
- **Oshi-hen (推し変):** Changing your favorite to a different member.   
- **Tan-ori (担降り):** Stopping your support for a specific member or "quitting" the fandom.   
   
### Fan Categories   
- **Wota (ヲタ):** Devoted idol fans (specifically spelled with ヲ to distinguish from general otaku).   
- **Onnawota (女ヲタ):** A female idol fan.   
- **Shinki (新規):** A new fan.   
- **Kosan (古参):** A veteran/long-time fan.   
- **TO (Top Otaku):** A fan leader who often coordinates chants and calls.   
- **Dou-tan (同担):** Fans who share the same favorite member.   
    - **Dou-tan Kyohi (同担拒否):** Fans who refuse to interact with others who like the same member.   
    - **Dou-tan Kangei (同担歓迎):** Fans who welcome others with the same favorite.   
- **Wotageishi (ヲタ芸師):** A fan who is highly skilled in performing *Wotagei* (fan dances).   
 --- 
   
## 🎤 The "Genba" (Live & Events)   
Terms used when attending concerts or meeting idols in person.   
### The Venue & Travel   
- **Genba (現場):** The "scene" or the actual venue where the event happens.   
- **Sansen (参戦):** "Going to war"—slang for attending a concert.   
- **Ensei (遠征):** Traveling a long distance (out of town) for a show.   
- **Zentsuu (全通):** Attending **every single date** of a concert tour.   
- **Saizen (最前):** The very front row.   
- **Kami-seki (神席):** "God seat"—an amazing seat with a perfect view.   
- **Hana-michi (花道):** The runway/bridge extending from the stage into the crowd.   
   
### Concert Rituals   
- **Wotagei (ヲタ芸):** The energetic, coordinated dances and arm movements performed by fans.   
- **Ouen Call / Chants (応援コール):** Planned shouts or responses during songs.   
- **Oshi Jump:** Jumping and pointing at your favorite member when they sing.   
- **Furimane (振りマネ):** Mimicking the idol's choreography from the crowd.   
- **Fansa (ファンサ):** Fan service; when an idol acknowledges a fan (waving, pointing, etc.).   
- **Ginte (銀テ):** Silver tape shot from cannons; highly collectible if they have signatures/logos.   
- **Setori (セトリ):** The Set List (order of songs).   
   
### Tickets & Sales   
- **Chuusen (抽選):** The lottery system used to buy tickets.   
- **Tou-raku (当落):** The "Win or Loss" result of the ticket lottery.   
- **Deji-chike (デジチケ):** Digital/mobile tickets.   
- **Kami-chike (紙チケ):** Physical paper tickets.   
 --- 
   
## 🛍️ Goods & Merch   
The items fans use to show their devotion.   
- **Penlight / Penra (ペンラ):** LED glowsticks.   
    - **Cyalume:** Chemical glowsticks (single-use).   
    - **UO (Ultra Orange):** High-intensity orange glowsticks used for "hype" moments.   
    - **KB (King Blade):** The most popular brand of professional penlight.   
- **Oshi Iro (推し色):** Your favorite member's designated color.   
- **Uchiwa (うちわ):** Large fans decorated with an idol's name or a request (e.g., "Wink at me!").   
- **Akusuta (アクスタ):** Acrylic stands of the members.   
- **Ita Bag (痛バッグ):** A "painful bag" covered in so many badges and keychains it looks heavy/crazy.   
- **Happi / Hachimaki:** Traditional Japanese coats and headbands worn by fans.   
- **Enban (円盤):** "The Disc"—referring to the Blu-ray or DVD release of a concert.   
- **Tokuten (特典):** Bonus items (photos, handshake tickets) included with a CD purchase.   
 --- 
   
## 🗣️ Common Buzzwords & Slang   
How fans describe their feelings and the idols' behavior.   
- **Toutoi (尊い):** "Precious" or "Sacred"—used when an idol is so beautiful/talented you can't handle it.   
- **Kami-taiou (神対応):** "God-level interaction"—when an idol is incredibly kind to fans.   
- **Shio-taiou (塩対応):** "Salt interaction"—when an idol is cold or indifferent (sometimes charming in its own way).   
- **Sotsugyo (卒業):** "Graduation"—when an idol leaves the group.   
- **Biju (ビジュ):** Short for "Visuals" (hair, makeup, outfit).   
- **Loss (ロス):** The "post-concert depression" or sadness after an idol leaves.   
- **Wakarimi ga fukai (わかりみが深い):** "The understanding is deep"—meaning "I totally relate to that."   
 --- 
   
### 📸 Interaction & Event Terms (The "Tokutenkai")   
When you go to a "Genba" (venue), the concert is only half the fun. These terms cover the meet-and-greet portion:   
- **Akushu-kai (握手会):** Handshake event.   
- **Cheki (チェキ):** An instant Polaroid photo taken with the idol.   
    - **2-Shot (2ショット):** A photo of you and the idol together.   
    - **Sign-ari / Sign-nashi:** A cheki "with" or "without" an autograph/message.   
- **Ninchi (認知):** "Recognition." When an idol officially remembers your face or name. This is the "Holy Grail" for many fans.   
- **Res (レス):** Short for "Response." When an idol makes eye contact, winks, or points at you *during* a song.   
- **Kamite (上手) & Shimote (下手):** Stage right and stage left. Fans often choose their standing spot based on which side their Oshi stands on most.   
- **Kagi-ake (鍵開け) / Kagi-shime (鍵締め):** Being the very **first** or very **last** person in line to talk to an idol at an event.   
 --- 
   
### 👹 Fan "Archetypes" (The Good & The Bad)   
- **Gachi-koi (ガチ恋):** Being "seriously in love" with the idol. These fans aren't just supporting; they genuinely want to date the member.   
- **Zaitaku (在宅):** "Homebody" fans. People who love the group but only watch videos/buy CDs and never actually go to live events.   
- **Jizo (地蔵):** Named after the Japanese stone statues. A fan who stands completely still and silent during a high-energy concert, usually just intensely observing.   
- **Omaitsu (オマイツ):** Slang for "You're always here, aren't you?" Used for super-fans who attend literally every single event.   
- **Riako (リアコ):** Similar to Gachi-koi; "Real Love." Having a "real-life" crush on the idol.   
 --- 
   
### 📣 Advanced Performance Terms   
- **MIX:** The specific, rhythmic English/Japanese/Ainu chants fans shout during song introductions (e.g., *"Tiger, Fire, Cyber, Fiber..."*).   
- **PPPH:** A classic cheer pattern (Pan-pa-pan-hyu!) usually done during the second verse of a song.   
- **Senbatsu (選抜):** The "Selected" members who get to perform the main song (common in large groups like AKB48).   
- **Kekka-happyo (結果発表):** The announcement of results (usually for popularity elections or auditions).   
 --- 
   
### 🎁 Niche Merch & Habits   
- **Haifu-gei (配布芸):** "The art of distribution." When a fan buys 100+ copies of the same CD to get handshake tickets and then hands out the extra CDs for free to strangers outside the venue.   
- **Oshi-mark (推しマーク):** A specific emoji fans put in their social media bios to show who they support (e.g., 🍎 for a member whose color is red or who likes apples).   
- **Seitansai (生誕祭):** A massive birthday festival organized by fans for a specific member.   
 --- 
   
### 🌑 The Underground (Chika) & Indie Scene   
In small "Live Houses," the rules are different and much more intimate.   
- **Buppan (物販):** Short for "goods sales." In the underground scene, this is when idols sit at a table after the show to sell merch and talk to fans.   
- **Taiban (対バン):** An event where multiple different idol groups perform one after another. It’s a "battle of the bands" style.   
- **One-Man (ワンマン):** A solo concert featuring only one group (a sign that they are becoming successful!).   
- **Kecak (ケチャ):** A specific move where fans crouch down and reach their arms out toward the idol in a "worshipping" motion during a slow or emotional part of a song.   
- **Lift (リフト):** When one fan lifts another fan onto their shoulders so they can be seen by the idol (Note: Often banned at major venues for safety!).   
 --- 
   
### 🇰🇷 The "K-Pop-ified" J-Pop Terms   
As groups like **NiziU**, **JO1**, and **ME:I** rise, Korean terminology has blended into the Japanese idol scene.   
- **Bias (バイアス):** Sometimes used instead of "Oshi," especially by younger fans.   
- **Bias Wrecker:** A member who makes you question your loyalty to your main Oshi.   
- **Chikkei (直筆):** Specifically refers to a "hand-signed" item (very common in photocard trading).   
- **Yontong (ヨントン):** A video call fan-sign event (from the Korean *yeongsang tonghwa*).   
- **Nugu (ヌグ):** Slang for a group that is "unknown" or has a very small following.   
 --- 
   
### ⚠️ The "Dark" & Extreme Terms   
Every fandom has its fringe elements. You'll see these in news reports or "venting" threads.   
- **Sasaeng / Stalker:** Fans who cross the line and follow idols into their private lives (homes, trains, etc.).   
- **Anti (アンチ):** Someone who actively hates a group and spreads rumors.   
- **Akgae (アクゲ):** A fan who likes only one member but **actively hates** all the other members of the same group.   
- **Enjou (炎上):** When an idol or a fan "goes up in flames" (gets cancelled or causes a massive scandal online).   
- **Ego-sa (エゴサ):** Short for "Ego-search." When idols search for their own names on Twitter/X to see what fans are saying.   
 --- 
   
### 📈 Modern "Oshikatsu" Activity Terms   
- **Numaru (沼る):** To "fall into the swamp." It means becoming so obsessed with an idol that you can't get out (spending all your money and time).   
- **Kyon (きゅん):** The sound of a heart fluttering. Used when an idol does something cute.   
- **Pien (ぴえん):** A cute way of saying "I'm crying" (usually from happiness or seeing something too precious).   
- **Riajuu (リア充):** Someone who is "satisfied with real life." Often used by otaku to describe people who *don't* have an obsessive idol hobby.   
- **Zaitaku (在宅):** A "stay-at-home" fan. Someone who supports from a distance (YouTube/Social Media) but never goes to live shows.   
 --- 
   
### 🎀 The "Idol Archetypes"   
- **Kusogaki (クソガキ):** A "bratty" idol who teases the fans or other members. It's usually an endearing term for a mischievous member.   
- **Ikebo (イケボ):** Short for "Ikemen Voice." Used for female idols with cool, deep, or "handsome" singing voices.   
- **Pon (ポン) / Ponkotsu:** A "clumsy" idol who forgets lyrics or trips on stage, making them seem more relatable and cute.
